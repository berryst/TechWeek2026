# Activity 4: 🎤 Talanoa with Copilot (Voice mode)

[← Back to Activities](../README.md)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Time** | 5 min |
| **Audience** | Years 3–6 |
| **Skill** | Voice prompting + critical thinking |
| **Tool** | Copilot voice (mobile app or web) |

> **Why it works:** Younger students engage faster with voice. The fact-check twist plants the most important AI-literacy lesson early.

## Step-by-step lab

1. Open Copilot voice mode on your device or use a shared device with your group.
2. Take turns asking Copilot one question using one of the example prompts or your own idea.
3. Listen carefully to Copilot's answer.
4. Ask yourself: "Do I think that is true?" and talk with your group about how you could check the answer.
## Prompt template

```text
Tell me a joke about a coconut and a computer.Explain how rainbows work like I'm 7.What would happen if dogs could talk for one day?Make up a song about Auckland.
```

**Sample prompt 1**

```text
Explain how volcanoes work like I’m 7.
```

**Sample prompt 2**

```text
Make up a silly song about South Auckland buses.
```

## Email it to yourself or your whanau for showing what you've accomplished

Share it via email by clicking the Share button in Copilot, selecting email, and entering the student or whānau email address.

## Learning outcome

AI is fast and helpful, but always check important facts with a grown-up or a trusted source.
