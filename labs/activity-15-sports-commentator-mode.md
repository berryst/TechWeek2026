# Activity 15: 🎙️ Sports Commentator Mode

[← Back to Activities](../README.md)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Time** | 5 min |
| **Audience** | Years 4–8 |
| **Skill** | Tone & voice prompting |
| **Tool** | Copilot (text) + Copilot voice for read-out |

> **Why it works:** Hooks the sports-loving kids who haven't engaged yet. They learn that 'tone' is a real prompt parameter and that Copilot can copy any style.

## Step-by-step lab

1. Write 3 short sentences about a real sporting moment you remember.
2. Use Prompt 1 to turn your moment into exciting live commentary.
3. Use Prompt 2 to rewrite the same moment in a completely different style.
4. Choose your favourite version and read it out with as much energy as you can.
## Prompt template

```text
PROMPT 1: Turn this sporting moment into 4 sentences of exciting live commentaryin the style of an NRL or rugby commentator. Add sound effects in CAPITAL LETTERS.Moment: [STUDENT'S 3 SENTENCES]PROMPT 2: Now redo the same moment but in the style of:[PICK ONE: David Attenborough nature doco / a hyped rapper /a calm BBC news anchor / a Pacific Island uncle telling a story].
```

**Sample prompt 1**

```text
Turn this sporting moment into 4 sentences of exciting live commentary in the style of a rugby commentator. Add sound effects in CAPITAL LETTERS. Moment: I caught the ball, ran past two defenders, and scored in the corner.
```

**Sample prompt 2**

```text
Now redo the same moment in the style of a calm BBC news anchor.
```

Tone is a prompt setting. The same facts can sound 10 different ways — that's a superpower.
