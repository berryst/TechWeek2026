# Activity 9: 🔍 Copilot Vision Adventure

[← Back to Activities](../README.md)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Time** | 5 min |
| **Audience** | Years 3–8 |
| **Skill** | Visual prompting + observation |
| **Tool** | Copilot Vision (mobile app — camera mode) |

> **Why it works:** Vision is the most jaw-dropping demo in the Copilot family. Kids see AI 'looking at' their world and instantly understand it's more than a chatbot.

## Step-by-step lab

1. Open Copilot Vision and point the camera at one object from your bag.
2. Ask Copilot what the object is and what it is used for.
3. Show Copilot a cultural object and ask what it can tell you about it.
4. Point the camera at some text and ask Copilot to translate it into another language.
5. Talk about what Copilot got right, what it got wrong, and why that might happen.
## Prompt template

```text
(Tap camera icon in Copilot mobile app, then speak the prompt)ROUND 1: "What is this object and what's it used for?"ROUND 2: "This is from my culture. What can you tell me about it?"Then: "Where is it traditionally from?"ROUND 3: "Read the words you can see and translate them into Samoan."(Or Tongan, te reo Māori, Tagalog — let kids pick.)
```

## Email it to yourself or your whanau for showing what you've accomplished

Share it via email by clicking the Share button in Copilot, selecting email, and entering the student or whānau email address.

## Learning outcome

Copilot Vision can see the world — but it doesn't always understand culture. You bring that knowledge.
